# Mastering Tofu

Tofu is an incredibly versatile protein that can absorb flavors beautifully when prepared correctly. The key to great tofu is proper pressing and cooking technique.

## Types of Tofu

- **Silken Tofu**: Soft, delicate texture perfect for smoothies, desserts, and soups
- **Soft Tofu**: Great for scrambles and gentle preparations
- **Firm Tofu**: The most versatile option for stir-fries, baking, and grilling
- **Extra Firm**: Best for crispy preparations and holds shape well

## Essential Techniques

### Pressing
Remove excess moisture by pressing tofu for 15-30 minutes. This allows it to absorb marinades and achieve a better texture when cooking.

### Marinating
Tofu readily absorbs flavors. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for maximum flavor.

### Cooking Methods
- **Pan-frying**: Creates a golden, crispy exterior
- **Baking**: Yields evenly cooked, firmer texture
- **Air-frying**: Quick and crispy with minimal oil
- **Scrambling**: Crumbled tofu makes an excellent egg substitute

## Pro Tips

1. Freeze and thaw tofu for a meatier, chewier texture
2. Cut tofu into cubes and toss with cornstarch before frying for extra crispiness
3. Don't overcrowd the pan - give tofu space to brown properly
4. Season generously - tofu needs bold flavors
