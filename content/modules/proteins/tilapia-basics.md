# Cooking Tilapia

Tilapia is one of the most accessible fish you can cook — affordable, mild-flavored, and incredibly versatile. Its firm white flesh takes on seasonings beautifully and cooks quickly, making it a great weeknight staple.

## Why Tilapia?

- **Mild flavor**: No strong "fishy" taste, making it approachable for hesitant fish-eaters
- **Quick cooking**: Fillets are done in under 10 minutes
- **Budget-friendly**: One of the most affordable fish available
- **Lean protein**: Low in fat, high in protein

## Selecting Tilapia

- Look for firm, translucent white flesh with no gray or yellow tinge
- Fresh fillets should smell clean and neutral, not fishy
- Frozen tilapia works very well — thaw overnight in the fridge or in cold water

## Cooking Methods

### Pan-Searing (Best for Crispy Results)
The go-to method for golden, restaurant-quality tilapia at home.

1. Pat fillets completely dry with paper towels — this is the most important step
2. Season both sides generously with salt, pepper, and your spice blend
3. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering
4. Place fillets in a single layer without crowding the pan
5. Cook 3–4 minutes per side until golden and opaque throughout
6. Internal temperature should reach 145°F (63°C)

### Oven Baking (Foolproof Method)
Great for cooking multiple fillets at once with minimal hands-on time.

- Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C)
- Place seasoned fillets in a greased baking dish
- Drizzle with lemon butter or olive oil
- Bake 10–12 minutes until fish flakes easily with a fork

### Blackened Tilapia
A bold, flavorful preparation with a smoky, spicy crust.

**Blackening Spice Mix:**
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp onion powder
- ½ tsp cumin
- ¼ tsp cayenne
- Salt and black pepper

Coat both sides of the fillet and pan-sear in a very hot cast iron skillet with a small amount of oil.

## Seasoning Ideas

- **Classic lemon-garlic butter**: Simple and always works
- **Mediterranean**: Olive oil, oregano, sun-dried tomatoes, capers
- **Soy-ginger glaze**: Soy sauce, honey, fresh ginger, sesame oil

### Taco-Seasoned Tilapia (for Fish Tacos)
A baked preparation that's perfect for fish tacos — the seasoning is bold without overpowering the fish, and the optional broil at the end gives you lightly caramelized edges.

**Seasoning (per 1½ lb of fillets):**
- ½ tsp cumin
- ½ tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp salt
- ¼ tsp black pepper

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 375°F
2. Season both sides of each fillet with the spice blend
3. Place in a greased baking dish
4. Drizzle lightly with olive oil and add a small pat of butter on each fillet
5. Bake for 20–25 minutes until fish flakes easily with a fork
6. Optional: broil for 3–5 minutes to create lightly browned edges
7. Break into chunks and serve in warm corn tortillas

## Key Tips

1. **Always dry the fish first** — moisture prevents browning
2. **Don't move it in the pan** — let it sear undisturbed for a proper crust
3. **Don't overcook** — tilapia is done the moment it flakes easily; beyond that it becomes dry and rubbery
4. **Hot pan, not hot oil** — heat the pan first, then add oil, then fish
