# Succotash Broth

Succotash — corn, beans, and summer vegetables — is typically a dry sauté, but adding a light broth transforms it into something more versatile: a warm, savory base that works as a stew, a sauce for grains, or a light summer soup. This broth is bright and vegetal, built on the sweetness of corn and the earthiness of beans, finished with herbs and a touch of acid.

## Why This Base Works

Traditional succotash is a side dish. This version treats it as a **broth-based preparation**, which opens up how the dish can be served:

- **Corn liquor** — the starchy water from blanching or steaming corn adds body without cream
- **Shell bean liquid** — if using fresh shell beans, the cooking liquid is deeply savory and should never be discarded
- **Smoked element** — a small amount of smoked paprika or a piece of smoked corn gives the broth dimension

The broth is intentionally light — it should taste like a summer garden, not a heavy stew.

## The Base Recipe

**Makes enough for one large pot (serves 4–6)**

### Ingredients

- 2 tbsp butter or olive oil
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced small
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced small
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp dried thyme
- 2 cups corn kernels (from about 3 ears; reserve any corn liquid from cutting)
- 1½ cups cooked or canned lima beans (or fresh shell beans with their liquid)
- 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
- 1 cup corn liquor (water from blanching corn, or water with 1 tsp reserved corn starch)
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp cider vinegar or fresh lemon juice
- ¼ cup fresh parsley or basil, roughly chopped

### Method

#### 1. Build the Aromatic Base
Melt butter in a wide, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add onion and cook 5–7 minutes until soft and lightly golden. Add garlic and bell pepper; cook 3 minutes more.

#### 2. Bloom the Spices
Add smoked paprika and thyme directly to the oil in the pan. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.

#### 3. Add Corn and Let It Color
Add corn kernels in a single layer (as much as possible). Let cook undisturbed for 2–3 minutes to develop light caramelization on some kernels. Stir and cook another 2 minutes.

#### 4. Add Beans and Liquid
Add lima beans (and any bean cooking liquid). Pour in broth and corn liquor. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer. Cook 10–12 minutes to marry the flavors.

#### 5. Finish
Stir in cider vinegar. Taste and adjust: more salt for flatness, more acid for brightness, a pinch of sugar if the corn isn't sweet. Remove from heat and stir in fresh herbs.

## Building a Full Succotash Stew

| Add-In | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Okra (sliced) | Last 8 min | Thickens the broth naturally |
| Cherry tomatoes (halved) | Last 5 min | Burst and sweeten the broth |
| Diced zucchini | Last 8 min | Stays tender without going mushy |
| Crab or shrimp | Last 3 min | Pairs naturally with corn; don't overcook |
| Andouille sausage (sliced, pre-browned) | First 5 min | Render before aromatics; use the fat |

## Regional Variations

Succotash has deep regional roots, and the broth can reflect different traditions:

- **Southern style** — add a smoked ham hock to the simmering broth; remove before serving
- **New England style** — stir in a splash of cream at the end; add salt cod if desired
- **Summer garden style** — skip paprika; finish with fresh basil and lemon zest

## Key Tips

1. **Use the bean liquid** — if cooking dried lima beans, that liquid is gold; use it as part of the broth
2. **Don't overcook the corn** — it should stay slightly crisp; overcooked corn turns rubbery
3. **Acid at the end** — vinegar or lemon brightens the whole pot; add early and it loses its edge
4. **Fresh herbs off heat** — stir in after removing from the burner to preserve their color and flavor
