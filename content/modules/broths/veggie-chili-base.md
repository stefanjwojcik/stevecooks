# Veggie Chili Base

A great veggie chili doesn't need meat — it needs depth. This base builds layers of flavor through toasted chili powder, smoky chipotle, and a tomato foundation that simmers into something rich and thick. The broth is bold enough to carry hearty beans and vegetables without tasting thin or one-dimensional.

## Why This Base Works

The trick is **blooming spices in fat** before adding any liquid. Dry chili powder and cumin hit hot oil and transform — their fat-soluble compounds release in a way that water-based simmering never achieves. The result is a broth that tastes like it cooked all day, even when it hasn't.

- **Chipotle in adobo** adds smokiness and a slow, building heat (not sharp like fresh chili)
- **Tomato paste** caramelizes against the pan, adding sweetness and body
- **Dried ancho or mulato chili** (optional but worth it) deepens the color and complexity

## The Base Recipe

**Makes enough for one large pot of chili (serves 6–8)**

### Ingredients

- 2 tbsp neutral oil (canola or safflower)
- 1 large yellow onion, diced
- 1 green bell pepper, diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1–2 chipotle peppers in adobo, minced (plus 1 tbsp adobo sauce)
- 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- 1 tsp kosher salt, plus more to taste
- 1 tsp sugar (balances acidity)

### Method

#### 1. Sweat the Aromatics
Heat oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion and bell pepper. Cook 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until soft and golden at the edges. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.

#### 2. Bloom the Spices
Push vegetables to the sides of the pot. Add chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and oregano directly to the oil in the center. Stir constantly for 60 seconds — the spices will darken slightly and become very fragrant. This is the most important step.

#### 3. Caramelize the Tomato Paste
Add tomato paste to the center of the pot. Let it sit undisturbed for 30 seconds, then stir it into the spices and vegetables. Cook another 2 minutes, scraping the bottom — you want it to deepen in color and start to stick slightly.

#### 4. Add the Liquid
Add chipotle peppers, adobo sauce, crushed tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Stir well, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Add salt and sugar. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer.

#### 5. Simmer and Adjust
Simmer uncovered for 20 minutes to concentrate the flavors. Taste and adjust: more chipotle for heat, salt for flatness, a splash of cider vinegar for brightness.

## Building the Full Chili

Add any of the following to the finished base:

| Add-In | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Canned black or kidney beans | Last 15 min | Rinse well; add two cans for a hearty pot |
| Corn kernels (fresh or frozen) | Last 10 min | Adds sweetness and texture |
| Diced zucchini | Last 10 min | Cooks quickly; don't add too early |
| Bulgur or farro | Simmer 20 min | Absorbs broth; add extra liquid |
| Sweet potato (cubed) | First 20 min | Needs time to soften |

## Toppings That Matter

The broth is the foundation — toppings finish it:
- **Sour cream or plain yogurt** — cools the heat, adds creaminess
- **Sharp cheddar** — melts into the broth
- **Pickled jalapeños** — acid cuts through richness
- **Cilantro and lime** — brightness
- **Avocado or guacamole** — fat and body

## Make-Ahead and Storage

The base improves overnight. Make it a day ahead, refrigerate, and reheat gently. It freezes well for up to 3 months — freeze before adding any delicate vegetables.
