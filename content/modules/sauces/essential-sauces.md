# Essential Sauces Across Cultures

Mastering a handful of essential sauces unlocks countless flavor possibilities and elevates simple ingredients into restaurant-quality meals.

## French Mother Sauces

### Béchamel (White Sauce)
Butter + flour (roux) + milk = creamy base for mac and cheese, lasagna, gratins

### Velouté
Roux + light stock (vegetable, fish) = delicate sauce for seafood

### Hollandaise
Egg yolks + butter + lemon = rich sauce for vegetables and fish

## Asian Essential Sauces

### Teriyaki
Soy sauce + mirin + sugar = sweet-savory glaze

**Quick Recipe:**
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 1/4 cup mirin
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 1 tsp grated ginger

### Stir-Fry Sauce
Soy sauce + rice vinegar + sesame oil + cornstarch

### Peanut Sauce
Peanut butter + soy sauce + lime + chili = versatile Asian sauce

## Mediterranean Sauces

### Tahini Sauce
Tahini + lemon + garlic + water = creamy Middle Eastern sauce

### Romesco
Roasted red peppers + almonds + garlic = Spanish versatility

### Chimichurri
Parsley + garlic + vinegar + oil = bright, herbaceous sauce

## Universal Sauce-Making Tips

1. **Emulsification**: Slowly add oil/butter while whisking
2. **Seasoning**: Taste and adjust salt, acid, sweetness
3. **Consistency**: Add water to thin, simmer to thicken
4. **Temperature**: Some sauces break if too hot (hollandaise, mayo)

## Building Flavor Layers

- **Base**: Oil or fat
- **Aromatics**: Garlic, ginger, shallots
- **Acid**: Vinegar, citrus, wine
- **Sweetness**: Sugar, honey, mirin
- **Umami**: Soy sauce, miso, fish sauce
- **Heat**: Chili, pepper, hot sauce
