# Rice Mastery

Rice is a staple grain enjoyed worldwide. Different varieties require different techniques, but once you understand the basics, you'll cook perfect rice every time.

## Types of Rice

### White Rice
- **Long-grain** (Basmati, Jasmine): Fluffy, separate grains
- **Medium-grain**: Slightly sticky, versatile
- **Short-grain**: Sticky, perfect for sushi

### Brown Rice
Nutritious whole grain with nutty flavor, requires longer cooking time.

### Specialty Rices
- **Arborio**: For creamy risotto
- **Wild rice**: Actually a grass, nutty and chewy
- **Black/Red rice**: Colorful, nutrient-dense varieties

## Basic Cooking Method

### Absorption Method (Most Common)

1. Rinse rice until water runs clear (removes excess starch)
2. Use ratio: 1 cup rice to 1.5-2 cups water (depends on variety)
3. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low simmer
4. Cover and cook: 15-18 min (white), 40-45 min (brown)
5. Let rest 5-10 minutes off heat
6. Fluff with a fork

### Rice Cooker Method
Foolproof and hands-off - just add rice and water per manufacturer instructions.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not rinsing**: Results in gummy, sticky rice
2. **Lifting the lid**: Releases steam and disrupts cooking
3. **Wrong water ratio**: Too much = mushy, too little = crunchy
4. **Skipping the rest**: Rice needs time to finish steaming

## Flavor Enhancements

- Cook in broth instead of water
- Add aromatics: garlic, bay leaf, onion
- Toast rice in oil before adding liquid
- Stir in herbs, lemon zest, or butter after cooking
