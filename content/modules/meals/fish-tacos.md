# Fish Tacos

A crowd-pleasing classic that brings together seasoned baked tilapia, fresh toppings, and a zesty lime crema — all wrapped in warm corn tortillas. This recipe makes 24 small tacos.

## Ingredients

### Fish
- 1½ lb tilapia fillets
- ½ tsp cumin
- ½ tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp salt
- ¼ tsp black pepper
- Olive oil
- Butter

### Tortillas
- 24 small white corn tortillas

### Toppings
- Purple cabbage, sliced thin
- 2 avocados, sliced
- Red onion, diced
- Fresh cilantro
- 1 cup cotija cheese, grated
- Roma tomatoes, diced (optional)
- Lime wedges

### Lime Crema Sauce
- ½ cup sour cream
- ⅓ cup mayonnaise
- 2 Tbsp lime juice
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp sriracha sauce

## Instructions

### 1. Make the Lime Crema
Whisk together sour cream, mayonnaise, lime juice, garlic powder, and sriracha until smooth. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

### 2. Prepare the Tilapia
1. Preheat oven to 375°F
2. Season both sides of each fillet with cumin, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper
3. Place fillets in a greased baking dish
4. Drizzle lightly with olive oil and add a small pat of butter on each fillet
5. Bake for 20–25 minutes until fish flakes easily with a fork
6. Optional: broil for 3–5 minutes to create lightly browned edges
7. Break fish into chunks for serving

### 3. Toast the Tortillas
Warm corn tortillas on a dry griddle or skillet over medium heat, about 30 seconds per side, until pliable and lightly toasted.

### 4. Assemble
Layer on each tortilla:
1. A portion of fish
2. Sliced purple cabbage
3. Avocado slices
4. Diced red onion
5. Fresh cilantro
6. Diced tomato (optional)
7. A generous sprinkle of cotija cheese
8. A drizzle of lime crema sauce

Serve with fresh lime wedges on the side.

## Tips

- **Don't skip the broil**: A few minutes under the broiler gives the fish great texture and lightly caramelized edges
- **Warm tortillas stay pliable**: Keep them wrapped in a clean towel after toasting so they don't crack when you fold them
- **Make the crema ahead**: The lime crema keeps in the fridge for up to 3 days and the flavor improves as it sits
- **Cotija is key**: Its salty, crumbly texture is what makes these tacos feel authentic — don't substitute with regular cheese if you can avoid it
