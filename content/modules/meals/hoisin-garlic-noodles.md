# Hoisin Garlic Noodles

Hoisin garlic noodles are a weeknight powerhouse — bold, savory, and ready in 20 minutes. The sauce is built on hoisin (a sweet-salty fermented soybean paste), toasted garlic, and a splash of sesame oil. This dish works beautifully as a standalone vegetarian meal or topped with shrimp, seared scallops, or crispy tofu.

## Understanding Hoisin Sauce

Hoisin is often called "Chinese BBQ sauce" — thick, dark, and deeply umami. It's made from fermented soybean paste, garlic, vinegar, sugar, and spices. Its combination of sweet, salty, and savory makes it a natural base for noodle sauces without needing a dozen ingredients.

**Don't substitute:** Oyster sauce and hoisin are not the same. Oyster sauce is thinner and fishier; hoisin is richer and sweeter.

## Choosing Your Noodles

| Noodle | Texture | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Lo mein / fresh egg noodles** | Soft, chewy | Best choice; absorbs sauce well |
| **Udon** | Thick, springy | Excellent — the chew holds up to the sauce |
| **Ramen noodles** | Thin, bouncy | Great weeknight option |
| **Spaghetti or linguine** | Firm | Works as a substitute; cook al dente |
| **Rice noodles** | Delicate | Use wide flat rice noodles; gluten-free option |

## The Recipe

**Serves 2–3 | Ready in 20 minutes**

### Ingredients

**Sauce:**
- 3 tbsp hoisin sauce
- 2 tbsp soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce (skip for fully vegetarian; add 1 tsp more hoisin)
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sugar or honey
- 2 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (optional, for a glossier sauce)

**Noodles:**
- 8 oz lo mein noodles, udon, or spaghetti
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (vegetable, canola)
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, minced (optional)
- 2 scallions, sliced (whites and greens separated)
- Red chili flakes or sambal oelek to taste

**To finish:**
- Sesame seeds
- Scallion greens
- Crispy shallots (optional)

**Optional protein:**
- 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 8 oz extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
- 4 scallops, patted dry

### Method

#### 1. Mix the Sauce
Whisk hoisin, soy sauce, oyster sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and sugar together in a small bowl. Taste and adjust — it should be savory, slightly sweet, with a hint of acid.

#### 2. Cook the Noodles
Cook noodles according to package directions, pulling them out 1 minute early (they'll finish in the sauce). Reserve ½ cup pasta water before draining. Toss drained noodles with a drizzle of sesame oil to prevent sticking.

#### 3. Bloom the Garlic
Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add scallion whites and garlic; stir constantly for 30–45 seconds until fragrant and just golden. Be careful — garlic burns quickly and turns bitter.

#### 4. Cook Protein (If Using)
Push aromatics to the side. Add shrimp or scallops to the hot pan; cook undisturbed 1–2 minutes per side until opaque. Or add tofu and sear until golden on two sides.

#### 5. Sauce the Noodles
Add noodles to the pan. Pour sauce over everything and toss vigorously with tongs. Add a splash of reserved pasta water to loosen if needed — the sauce should coat every strand. Cook 1–2 minutes until glossy.

#### 6. Serve
Pile into bowls, top with sesame seeds, scallion greens, and crispy shallots if using.

## Variations

### Spicy Hoisin Noodles
Add 1–2 tbsp chili garlic sauce or gochujang to the sauce. Finish with a drizzle of chili oil.

### Peanut Hoisin Noodles
Whisk 2 tbsp peanut butter into the sauce. Add ¼ cup warm water to thin. Garnish with crushed peanuts and cucumber ribbons.

### Sesame Cucumber Topping
Thinly slice half a cucumber and toss with rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, and chili flakes. Pile on top of noodles just before serving — the cool crunch balances the rich sauce.

## Key Tips

1. **Reserve pasta water** — the starch helps the sauce cling and emulsify
2. **High heat, fast cook** — this dish moves quickly; have everything prepped before you turn on the heat
3. **Don't crowd the protein** — if cooking shrimp or scallops, give them space to sear, not steam
4. **Taste the sauce before adding** — hoisin and soy vary by brand; adjust sweetness and salt to your palate
