# Curried Coconut and Red Lentil Soup

This is a deeply satisfying, naturally vegan soup that tastes like it took hours but comes together in under 45 minutes. Red lentils dissolve as they cook, creating a thick, creamy texture without any blending. The base is a classic mirepoix (onion, celery, carrot) boosted by ginger and garlic, finished with coconut milk and curry powder. A spoonful of chile crisp and a squeeze of lime at the table make it complete.

## Why Red Lentils?

Red lentils are the fastest-cooking legume in your pantry:
- They soften and break down in 15–20 minutes (no soaking required)
- As they cook, they lose their shape and thicken the broth naturally
- They have a mild, slightly earthy flavor that absorbs curry spices beautifully

**Do not substitute green or brown lentils** — they hold their shape and won't create the creamy texture this soup depends on.

## Curry Powder: What to Look For

This recipe specifies **Madras curry powder**, which is a blend of coriander, cumin, turmeric, chili, and other spices with a moderate heat level and warm complexity. It's more intense than mild grocery-store blends.

- **Madras** — earthier, slightly hotter, more complex
- **Generic curry powder** — milder, good starting point; use a full tablespoon
- **Garam masala** — warmer and sweeter; pair with ½ tsp turmeric if substituting

Toast your curry powder in oil before adding liquid — this is what takes the soup from flat to aromatic.

## The Recipe

**Serves 4–6**

### Ingredients

- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large yellow onion, roughly chopped
- 3 celery ribs, roughly chopped
- 1 large carrot, roughly chopped
- 5 garlic cloves, smashed and peeled
- 1 (2-inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
- 1 tbsp kosher salt (Diamond Crystal; use half if using Morton or fine salt)
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tbsp Madras curry powder
- 4 cups vegetable broth
- 1 (13½ oz) can full-fat coconut milk
- 1 cup red lentils, rinsed well
- 1 cup coconut water (optional — adds subtle sweetness and thins the soup slightly)
- Chile crisp, for serving
- Lime or lemon wedges, for serving

### Method

#### 1. Sweat the Vegetables
Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion, celery, and carrot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent — about 8–10 minutes. Don't rush this; the vegetable base is the soup's backbone.

#### 2. Add Aromatics
Add garlic and ginger. Cook, stirring, for 2 minutes until fragrant. Season with the tablespoon of salt.

#### 3. Toast Paste and Spices
Add tomato paste to the pot and stir into the vegetables. Cook 1–2 minutes until it darkens slightly. Add curry powder and stir to coat everything; cook 1 minute more, until the spices smell toasty. This step is critical — it blooms the spices in oil before liquid dilutes them.

#### 4. Add Liquids and Lentils
Pour in vegetable broth, coconut milk, and coconut water (if using). Stir to combine. Add rinsed lentils. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a steady simmer.

#### 5. Simmer Until Lentils Dissolve
Cook uncovered, stirring occasionally, for 20–25 minutes. The lentils will go from firm to soft to nearly dissolved — the soup should thicken noticeably. If it gets too thick, add a splash of water or broth.

#### 6. Blend (Optional)
For a completely smooth soup: use an immersion blender directly in the pot, or transfer in batches to a stand blender. For a more rustic texture, leave it as-is.

#### 7. Taste and Serve
Taste and adjust: more salt, more curry powder, or a squeeze of lime. Ladle into bowls and top with a spoonful of chile crisp. Serve with lime or lemon wedges.

## The Role of Coconut Water

Coconut water is optional but interesting:
- It adds a very subtle sweetness that complements the curry without the richness of coconut milk
- It thins the soup without diluting flavor the way plain water does
- Omit it if you want a thicker, richer soup; use it if you prefer a more brothy consistency

## Chile Crisp: What It Is and How to Use It

Chile crisp (or chili crisp) is a Chinese condiment of fried chilies and aromatics in oil — crunchy, spicy, and deeply savory. The Lao Gan Ma brand is the most widely available. It's an excellent finishing oil for soups, noodles, and eggs.

- **Spoon it on top** just before eating — don't stir it in or the crunch is lost
- Start with ½ tsp per bowl; it's more intense than it looks

## Serving Suggestions

- **With crusty bread** — for dipping into the thick, creamy broth
- **Over rice** — a scoop of basmati makes it more substantial
- **With a fried egg** — slide a fried or soft-boiled egg on top for protein
- **With naan** — warm naan soaks up the curry-coconut broth beautifully

## Make-Ahead and Storage

This soup keeps well and often tastes better the next day as the flavors deepen:
- **Refrigerator:** 4–5 days in an airtight container
- **Freezer:** Up to 3 months; the lentils hold up well to freezing
- **Reheating:** Add a splash of water or broth when reheating — the soup thickens significantly as it cools

## Key Tips

1. **Rinse the lentils** — rinse under cold water until it runs clear; removes surface starch and any debris
2. **Diamond Crystal vs. Morton salt** — Diamond Crystal is lighter by volume; if using Morton or fine sea salt, use half the amount
3. **Full-fat coconut milk** — light coconut milk produces a thinner, less satisfying soup
4. **Bloom the spices** — adding curry powder before the liquid and letting it cook in oil makes a dramatically more flavorful soup
5. **Don't skip the acid** — lime or lemon at serving lifts all the rich, creamy flavors and makes the soup feel balanced
