# Pescatarian Paella

Paella is the ultimate "bringing it all together" dish — a single pan meal that draws on your rice technique, your understanding of seafood proteins, and your ability to build deep flavor. This pescatarian version uses shrimp, mussels, clams, and squid over a saffron-scented bomba rice base. It's a party dish, a weeknight showstopper, and a lesson in patience.

## What Makes Paella, Paella

Authentic paella is defined by three things:

1. **The socarrat** — the caramelized, slightly crispy layer of rice on the bottom of the pan. This is the most prized part.
2. **The rice** — short-grain bomba or Calasparra rice, which absorbs liquid without going mushy.
3. **The sofrito** — a slow-cooked base of tomatoes, garlic, and onion that gives the dish its backbone.

Paella is not stirred like risotto. Once the broth goes in, you leave it alone and let the bottom toast.

## Choosing Your Seafood

For a pescatarian paella, use a combination that provides different textures and flavors:

| Seafood | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Mussels** | Visual drama, briny flavor | Add in the last 8–10 min |
| **Clams** | Briny depth | Add at the same time as mussels |
| **Shrimp** | Meaty, crowd-pleasing | Add last 5 min, shell-on for flavor |
| **Squid** | Chew and umami | Sauté in sofrito early, remove before adding rice |
| **Monkfish / Halibut** | Firm, holds its shape | Add with shrimp |

You don't need all of these — mussels + shrimp is a perfectly complete paella.

## Essential Equipment

- **Paella pan** (or the widest, flattest pan you own — a 12" or 14" skillet works)
- Wide surface area is key: more rice in contact with the bottom = more socarrat

## The Recipe

**Serves 4–6**

### Ingredients

**For the sofrito:**
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
- 4 ripe Roma tomatoes, grated or blended (or one 14 oz can crushed tomatoes)
- 1 tsp sweet smoked paprika (pimentón dulce)
- Salt to taste

**For the paella:**
- 2 cups bomba rice (or Calasparra; substitute Arborio if unavailable)
- 4½ cups seafood stock or fish broth (warm)
- ½ tsp saffron threads, steeped in ¼ cup warm water for 10 minutes
- 1 lb mussels, scrubbed and debearded
- 1 lb littleneck clams, scrubbed
- ¾ lb large shrimp, shell-on (deveined)
- ½ lb squid, cleaned and cut into rings (optional)
- Lemon wedges, fresh parsley, for serving

### Method

#### 1. Make the Sofrito
Heat olive oil in your paella pan over medium heat. Add onion and bell pepper; cook 8–10 minutes until soft. Add garlic and cook 1 minute. Add tomatoes and smoked paprika. Cook, stirring frequently, until the mixture is thick and deeply caramelized — 12–15 minutes. This is the most important step; don't rush it.

#### 2. Toast the Rice
Add the rice directly to the sofrito and stir to coat each grain. Toast for 1–2 minutes.

#### 3. Add Liquid
Pour in the warm seafood stock and the saffron with its soaking liquid. Stir once to distribute evenly, then **do not stir again**. Season with salt. Bring to a vigorous boil, then reduce to a medium simmer.

#### 4. Add Seafood in Stages
- At **8 minutes**: nestle mussels and clams hinge-side down into the rice; press in gently
- At **12 minutes**: add shrimp around the pan, pressing them into the rice
- Cook until all shellfish have opened and shrimp are pink — about 4–5 more minutes

#### 5. Create the Socarrat
In the last 2 minutes, turn the heat to medium-high. You should hear a faint crackling — that's the socarrat forming. Watch carefully; you want toasted, not burned. Remove any unopened shells.

#### 6. Rest and Serve
Remove from heat, cover with foil or a clean towel, and rest for 5 minutes. Squeeze lemon over the top, scatter fresh parsley, and serve directly from the pan.

## Socarrat Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No socarrat | Heat too low, or pan too small | Increase heat in last 2 min; use wider pan |
| Burned bottom | Heat too high, rice too thin | Reduce heat; use more rice |
| Rice mushy | Too much liquid or stirring | Use bomba rice (more forgiving); don't stir |
| Rice undercooked | Not enough liquid or heat | Add small splash of broth, cover, cook 2 more min |

## Making Seafood Stock from Scratch

Paella is better with homemade seafood stock. Use the shells from your shrimp, plus:
- Shrimp shells + heads (if you have them)
- Fish scraps or a fish frame
- Onion, celery, bay leaf, peppercorns, white wine
- Simmer 20–25 minutes (don't over-cook — fish stock turns bitter)
- Strain and use immediately

## Key Tips

1. **Use warm stock** — cold stock drops the pan temperature and disrupts cooking
2. **Don't stir after adding liquid** — stirring releases starch and makes it gummy, not socarrat
3. **Shell-on shrimp** — the shells add flavor and protect the shrimp from overcooking
4. **Discard closed shells** — any mussel or clam that doesn't open after cooking should be discarded
5. **Saffron is non-negotiable** — it's expensive, but even a small pinch transforms the flavor and color
