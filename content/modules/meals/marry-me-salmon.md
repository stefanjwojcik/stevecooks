# Marry Me Salmon

"Marry Me" salmon is a riff on the viral "Marry Me Chicken" — a rich, Italian-inspired pan sauce built on sun-dried tomatoes, cream, clam juice, and oregano. The result is a savory, slightly tangy braise that's dramatic enough to impress at a dinner party but easy enough for a weeknight. The clam juice adds a subtle oceanic depth that ties the sauce to the salmon beautifully.

## Why Clam Juice?

Clam juice is bottled clam broth — lightly briny and savory without tasting strongly of clams. It's one of the best pantry shortcuts for pescatarian cooking because it:
- Adds seafood flavor without any prep
- Deepens pan sauces and chowders
- Works anywhere you'd use chicken stock in a seafood context

Find it in the canned fish/seafood aisle. Once opened, use within a few days.

## Sun-Dried Tomatoes: Oil-Packed vs. Dry

This recipe calls for **oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes**, and the jar oil is used for cooking — don't skip this. The oil is infused with concentrated tomato flavor, garlic, and herbs, and forms the base of the sauce. Dry-packed tomatoes are fine but need to be rehydrated and lack the flavored oil.

## The Recipe

**Serves 2**

### Ingredients

- 2 salmon fillets, preferably skin-on (10 oz total)
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- ¼ cup thinly sliced oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes, plus 2 tbsp oil from the jar
- ½ medium yellow onion, finely chopped
- 1 tsp dried oregano, plus more to taste
- ½ to 1 tsp crushed red pepper, plus more to taste
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 (8 oz) bottle clam juice (1 cup)
- ½ cup heavy cream
- Fresh basil leaves, for serving (optional)

### Method

#### 1. Season and Sear the Salmon
Pat salmon fillets dry with paper towels. Season generously on both sides with salt and pepper. Heat the sun-dried tomato oil in a skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. Place salmon skin-side up and sear 3–4 minutes without moving, until a golden crust forms. Flip and sear 2 minutes more. Transfer to a plate — the salmon will finish in the sauce.

#### 2. Build the Base
Reduce heat to medium. Add onion to the same pan (with remaining oil, adding a drizzle more if needed). Cook, stirring, until softened — about 4 minutes. Add sun-dried tomatoes, oregano, and red pepper flakes; stir for 1 minute.

#### 3. Add Tomato Paste
Push aromatics to the side and add tomato paste directly to the pan. Cook, stirring the paste into the pan surface, for 1–2 minutes until it darkens slightly. This caramelizes the tomato paste and deepens the sauce's flavor.

#### 4. Deglaze and Simmer
Pour in the clam juice, scraping up any browned bits from the pan bottom. Bring to a simmer and cook until reduced by about a third — 3–4 minutes. Add heavy cream and stir to combine. Simmer 2 minutes until the sauce thickens to coat a spoon.

#### 5. Finish the Salmon
Return salmon fillets to the pan, skin-side down. Spoon sauce over the fish. Cover and cook over medium-low heat for 3–4 minutes, until salmon is cooked through (it should flake easily at the thickest point and be opaque throughout, or slightly translucent in the center if you prefer medium).

#### 6. Serve
Taste sauce and adjust — more red pepper for heat, a pinch of oregano, salt as needed. Serve directly from the pan with fresh basil torn over the top. Good with crusty bread, pasta, or polenta to soak up the sauce.

## Reading Salmon Doneness

| Internal Temp | Texture | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 110–120°F | Silky, translucent center | Medium-rare; preferred by many cooks |
| 125–130°F | Just set, slightly translucent | Medium; ideal for this dish |
| 140°F+ | Fully opaque, firm | Well done; drier |

Use an instant-read thermometer inserted into the thickest part if you're unsure.

## Variations

### Over Pasta
Cook linguine or spaghetti while the sauce simmers. Toss pasta with sauce, then flake salmon over the top. A complete meal in one pan.

### Lighten the Sauce
Substitute half-and-half for heavy cream, or use 2 tbsp cream and ¼ cup clam juice for a thinner, brighter sauce.

### Add Capers or Olives
Stir in 1 tbsp drained capers or a handful of sliced Kalamata olives when adding the clam juice. The brininess pairs well with the cream.

## Key Tips

1. **Use the jar oil** — it's seasoned and flavored; don't substitute plain olive oil
2. **Sear first, finish in sauce** — searing builds flavor and gives the salmon structure before the braise
3. **Don't overcrowd the pan** — use a pan wide enough for both fillets in a single layer
4. **Cook tomato paste until it darkens** — this removes the raw, metallic edge and adds caramel depth
5. **Skin-on fillets** — the skin protects the salmon from overcooking on the bottom during the braise
