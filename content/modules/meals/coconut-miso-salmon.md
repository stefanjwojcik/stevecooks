# Coconut Miso Salmon

This one-pan dish brings together two umami powerhouses — white miso and coconut milk — into a silky, gingery broth that gently braises salmon until it's just set. Wilted spinach and a hit of lime juice keep it bright. Serve over jasmine or basmati rice to catch every drop of the broth.

## Why This Dish Works

Miso and coconut milk are a natural pairing:
- **White miso** (shiro miso) is mild, sweet, and rich with fermented soybean flavor
- **Coconut milk** contributes fat and sweetness that rounds out the miso's saltiness
- Together they create a broth that's savory without being heavy — more elegant than either ingredient alone

The salmon is **braised** in this sauce, not seared, which means it stays tender and absorbs the aromatics as it cooks.

## The Recipe

**Serves 4**

### Ingredients

- 2 tbsp safflower or canola oil
- 1 medium red onion, halved and sliced ½-inch thick (about 2 cups)
- 1 (1-inch) piece fresh ginger, minced (about 2 tablespoons)
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- ¼ cup white miso
- ½ cup unsweetened, full-fat canned coconut milk
- 1 (1½-pound) salmon fillet, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 5 oz baby spinach (about 5 packed cups)
- 1 tbsp fresh lime juice, plus lime wedges for serving
- Steamed jasmine or basmati rice, for serving
- ¼ cup chopped fresh basil
- ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro

### Method

#### 1. Sauté the Aromatics
Heat oil in a large, wide skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add onion in a single layer and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened and beginning to color at the edges — about 6–8 minutes. Add ginger and garlic; cook 1–2 minutes more until fragrant. Season with salt and pepper.

#### 2. Build the Sauce
In a small bowl, whisk miso with coconut milk until fully dissolved and smooth — miso can be lumpy, so whisk thoroughly. Pour into the pan and stir to combine with the aromatics. Bring to a gentle simmer.

#### 3. Braise the Salmon
Season salmon pieces with salt and pepper. Nestle them into the simmering sauce in a single layer. Cover the pan and cook over medium-low heat for 5–7 minutes, until the salmon is just cooked through — it should still be slightly translucent at the very center when you check with a fork (carryover heat will finish it). Do not boil; a gentle simmer keeps the salmon tender.

#### 4. Wilt the Spinach
Uncover the pan and add spinach in large handfuls, folding it into the broth. It will wilt down quickly — 1–2 minutes. The sauce should be brothy but coating.

#### 5. Finish and Serve
Remove from heat. Stir in lime juice. Taste the broth and adjust: more lime for brightness, a pinch of salt if flat, a dash of miso if it needs depth.

Serve over rice in wide bowls, spooning plenty of broth over everything. Top with basil, cilantro, and lime wedges on the side.

## Miso Primer

| Miso Type | Color | Flavor | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **White (shiro)** | Pale yellow | Mild, sweet, delicate | This dish; dressings, light soups |
| **Yellow (shinshu)** | Golden | Medium, slightly earthy | Miso soup, marinades |
| **Red (aka)** | Dark brown/red | Bold, intense, salty | Braises, ramen, hearty soups |

For this recipe, white miso is essential — red miso will overpower the coconut milk and make the broth too assertive.

## Variations

### Swap the Greens
Baby bok choy (halved) or kale (stems removed, roughly torn) both work well in place of spinach. Bok choy will take 3–4 minutes to soften; add it before the salmon.

### Add Noodles
For a heartier meal, cook soba or udon noodles separately and add them to the bowl before ladling the broth over. The broth doubles as a noodle soup base.

### Make It Vegetarian
Replace salmon with cubed extra-firm tofu (pressed and pan-fried first) or thick slices of eggplant. Double the coconut milk for a saucier base.

## Key Tips

1. **Dissolve the miso fully** — whisk it with coconut milk before adding to the pan; clumps of miso won't distribute evenly
2. **Don't boil the salmon** — a vigorous boil makes salmon grainy and dry; keep it at a gentle simmer
3. **Fresh lime at the end** — lime added early loses its brightness; add just before serving
4. **Cut salmon evenly** — uniform 2-inch pieces cook at the same rate; uneven pieces mean some overcooked, some raw
5. **Full-fat coconut milk** — light coconut milk produces a watery, less satisfying broth
